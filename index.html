The Coupling Challenge

What is Coupling?

  • Degree of interdependence between components
  • Essential for communication but must be managed
  • Not about elimination - about intentional design

Tight Coupling

  • Rigid structures
  • Change propagation
  • Coordination overhead

Loose Coupling

  • Flexible interfaces
  • Independent evolution
  • Team autonomy

Why Coupling Matters

Speed

How quickly can teams deliver value?

Reliability

Predictability of system behavior

Scalability

Ability to grow teams and systems

Maintainability

Cost of changes over time

"Big Ball of Mud" anti-pattern emerges when coupling is ignored

Brooks' Law: Adding people to coupled systems increases delivery time

Scaling Strategies

Coordinated Approach

  • Shared codebase
  • Continuous Integration
  • Fast feedback loops
  • Heavy automation

Distributed Approach

  • Decoupled systems
  • Microservices architecture
  • Independent deployability
  • Team ownership

Microservices are primarily an organizational scaling pattern

Key benefit: Independent deployability

Coupling Nuances

DRY Principle Reconsidered

  • Within bounded contexts: Essential
  • Between services: Dangerous
  • Cost of coupling > Cost of duplication

Communication Patterns

  • Synchronous: Simpler but creates runtime coupling
  • Asynchronous: More resilient but complex

Testability as a compass: Hard-to-test code often indicates problematic coupling
